3. EU AI ACT — PROHIBITED PRACTICES

In compliance with the Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689), the Customer shall not use the Agentic Service for or in connection with any prohibited AI practice. In particular, the Customer shall not:

(a) deploy subliminal, manipulative, or deceptive techniques that distort a person's behaviour in a manner that causes or is likely to cause that person or another person physical or psychological harm;

(b) exploit any vulnerabilities of a person or group related to age, disability, or socio-economic circumstances to materially distort their behaviour in a manner that causes or is likely to cause harm;

(c) evaluate or classify individuals based on their social behaviour or personal traits (social scoring), where such scoring leads to detrimental or unfavourable treatment unrelated to the context in which the data was originally generated;

(d) assess or predict the risk of an individual committing a criminal offence based solely on profiling or personal traits;

(e) create or expand facial recognition databases through untargeted scraping of facial images;

(f) conduct real-time remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces for the purpose of law enforcement;

(g) infer the emotions of individuals in the workplace or educational settings, except where such use is necessary for medical or safety reasons; or

(h) categorise individuals based on biometric data to deduce or infer sensitive attributes such as race, political opinions, trade union membership, religious or philosophical beliefs, sex life, or sexual orientation.
